Paro Vally

khangkhu

Paro is one of the most beautiful and culturally rich valleys in Bhutan, known for its peaceful atmosphere, stunning landscapes, and deep spiritual heritage. Surrounded by green mountains, rice fields, and traditional Bhutanese homes, Paro feels calm and timeless compared to busy modern cities. It is home to some of Bhutan’s most important landmarks, including the iconic Paro Taktsang (Tiger’s Nest Monastery), dramatically built on a cliffside high above the valley. This sacred site attracts both pilgrims and travelers from around the world. Paro also has the historic Paro Dzong, which stands as a symbol of Bhutanese architecture and history. The valley is also famous for its clean air, scenic drives, and the Paro International Airport, one of the most challenging and scenic airports in the world due to its location between mountains. Overall, Paro represents the heart of Bhutanese culture—peaceful, spiritual, and naturally breathtaking.
